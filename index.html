The Problem

Your best people are buried in busywork

You didn't build your company to spend weekends on spreadsheets. But that's where the hours go.

Manual estimating

Every bid means copying data across dozens of tabs, re-entering the same numbers, and hoping nothing slips through the cracks before the deadline.

Scattered historical data

Past project costs live in Excel files scattered across your server. Nobody has time to build a real database, so every bid starts from scratch.

Tight turnarounds

Two weeks to turn a bid. Your estimators are working nights and weekends just to keep up, and you're leaving money on the table when they rush.

No visibility into costs

You know what you bid. You know what you spent. But connecting those two numbers across projects? That takes a consultant and a weekend.

I've always had a deep respect for smaller companies. Building something from the ground up is hard work, and the people who do it deliver an incredible service and build a culture that big companies can only dream of. But too often, they're buried in manual work that eats into their bottom line. That's why I started Berlin Management Group, because a small company's bottom line matters just as much as anyone else's.
